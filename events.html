Awareness in Motion with the Alexander Technique

Workshops: Wednesday, March 16, 7:00 pm – 8:30 pm

Fee: $15
Beth Robinson, Certified Alexander Technique Teacher.

Our bodies are designed to move with energy, balance, and ease. Too often, our lifestyles interfere with this design, resulting in tension, unnecessary effort, and fatigue.

Learn how our every day movements, such as sitting down, standing up, and walking, may create unnecessary tension in our bodies. Through increased awareness, and gentle hands-on guidance from the teacher, we create the possibility for change.

The Alexander Technique allows us the opportunity to recognize and even undo habits that often lead to strain and stress.

The Alexander Technique is a proven, safe, self care method to stop pain, stress and muscular tension caused by everyday misuse of the body. People who learn this unique, sophisticated method can enhance their movement, function, and performance skills. You can apply what you learn through Alexander Technique to improved use of the body in such areas as instrumental and singing techniques, dance, athletics, and daily life activities.

“The Alexander Technique doesn’t teach you something new to do. It teaches you how to bring more practical intelligence into what you are already doing; how to eliminate stereotyped responses; how to deal with habit and change. It leaves you free to choose your own goal but gives you a better use of yourself while you work toward it.” Frank Pierce Jones, Body Awareness in Action

What can it do for You?

  • Reduce stress, muscular tension, and anxiety
  • Improve posture, balance, and coordination
  • Prevent habits that can lead to injury
  • Discover increased agility, energy, and poise
  • Increase overall awareness and capacity for concentration
  • Maintain a healthy back

Who studies the Alexander Technique?

  • Performing artists
  • Athletes and martial artists
  • Computer users
  • Public speakers and business professionals
  • Anyone with physical or emotional discomfort due to stress, postural habits, or movement problems

Beth Robinson is a Certified Alexander Technique Teacher. She received her training at the Montreal School of the Alexander Technique in December 2004. A member of the American Society for the Alexander Technique (AmSAT), Beth has worked with musicians, actors, dancers, athletes, office workers, seniors, children, and pregnant and new mothers.

Workshops and classes are an excellent introduction to the Alexander Technique. The sessions are open to anyone, and will include both group and individual attention. Participants should wear comfortable clothing. A yoga mat is helpful, but not required.

What walks on four legs in the morning, two legs in the afternoon and three legs in the evening?

--The Riddle of the Sphinx

How is it that people learn to move and walk on two legs and yet over time, lose the ability to walk upright and wind up walking with a cane or some form of support? From this perspective we may think that aging or growing older means to become debilitated or crippled. But is that really true?

What if the idea of aging was simply a myth and we could learn to move easily without pain and stiffness well into our golden years?

Students will explore and expose the myth of aging. Using innovative research and techniques, you’ll learn how to reverse the aging process and what it actually means to age. We’ll look at why we continue to believe something that simply isn’t true and how we are socialized to believe the myth of aging.

In addition, we’ll practice a series of movements that will relax your muscles and leave you feeling centered in your body and calm in your mind. You will notice a marked difference in the way you feel and move.

If you want to slow down your aging process and learn to move fluidly in your body, then this somatics is for you!

Richard’s bio:

An educator with a variety of teaching experience, Richard has explored Somatic movement for over 10 years. He completed 2 years of Somatic Experiencing, a form of trauma training, and spent over 8 years studying Hanna Somatic Education. Richard is also certified Somayog teacher, a style of yoga, which uses Somatic movement and yogic breathing.

He is inspired by the Somatic perspective of less is more. Richard encourages students to explore how they move and to be attentive while they practice. Through this, students discover their felt sense and greater body awareness
