What walks on four legs in the morning, two legs in the afternoon and three legs in the evening?

--The Riddle of the Sphinx

How is it that people learn to move and walk on two legs and yet over time, lose the ability to walk upright and wind up walking with a cane or some form of support? From this perspective we may think that aging or growing older means to become debilitated or crippled. But is that really true?

What if the idea of aging was simply a myth and we could learn to move easily without pain and stiffness well into our golden years?

This workshop will explore and expose the myth of aging. Using innovative research and techniques, you’ll learn how to reverse the aging process and what it actually means to age. We’ll look at why we continue to believe something that simply isn’t true and how we are socialized to believe the myth of aging.

In addition, we’ll practice a series of movements that will relax your muscles and leave you feeling centered in your body and calm in your mind. No matter how you feel at the start of the workshop, by the end you will notice a marked difference in the way you feel and move.

If you want to slow down your aging process and learn to move fluidly in your body, then this workshop is you!

Richard Hudspith: An educator with a variety of teaching experience, Richard has explored Somatic movement for over 10 years. He completed 2 years of Somatic Experiencing, a form of trauma training, and spent over 8 years studying Hanna Somatic Education. Richard is also certified Somayog teacher, a style of yoga, which uses Somatic movement and yogic breathing.

He is inspired by the Somatic perspective of less is more. Richard encourages students to explore how they move and to be attentive while they practice. Through this, students discover their felt sense and greater body awareness

A lot of us are clairsentient, which means that we pick up on the feelings of those around us. Sometimes this is a good thing and sometimes it's too much of a good thing. This retreat is a way to explore ways (both energetically and more concretely) to create boundaries around our innate abilities. If you feel that you sometimes take on the pain of others, this retreat/workshop will help you. We will be doing a combination of dialog, movement, teaching sessions, guided meditations, and creative expression. You will learn skills for what to do before difficult or emotional encounters, during the encounters, and afterwards if you feel drained.

For sensitive people, the chance to come into community with others who are like themselves can be invaluable. We learn from each other and feel supported in a very rich and empowering way.
